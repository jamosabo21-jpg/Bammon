An asymptote, in mathematics, is a straight line on a graph that a curve approaches indefinitely, getting closer and closer as it progresses, without ever crossing it.

Understanding the asymptote as the level of perfection, Bammon arises from the will to bring the practice of design, especially object design, to an asymptotically close level to perfection, without ever reaching it no matter how close it gets.

The game of backgammon, chosen merely as a vehicle to pursue the ideal, offers a combination of top-notch materials, treated and worked by artisan hands.


It is in that small percentage of imperfection, in not crossing the asymptote of the perfect, where those witnesses of the artisanal hands fit, reflected in imperfect details, which paradoxically continue to give meaning to the ultimate quest: a well-executed task.
